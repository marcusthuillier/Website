Entering the Data Center Ecosystem
MBA Consultant · Strategic Framework for a National Bank
As part of Stern's project-based experiential learning program, served as MBA Consultant to a National Bank exploring entry into the rapidly growing data center financing market — driven by AI infrastructure buildout and hyperscaler demand.
Constructed a strategic framework covering: target industries within the data center ecosystem, financing approaches (construction lending, equipment financing, sale-leaseback), product deployment strategies aligned to the bank's risk appetite, and advisory service offerings to differentiate beyond pure capital provision.
Market Entry Strategy
Mapped the data center financing landscape — hyperscaler demand, colocation, edge computing — and identified target segments for the bank's risk profile.
Financing Structures
Analyzed construction lending, equipment financing, and sale-leaseback structures across the data center lifecycle.
Product Roadmap
Recommended sequenced product deployment — from secured lending to advisory services — aligned to the bank's capabilities and appetite.
Competitive Differentiation
Advisory and data-informed services to differentiate beyond capital provision in an increasingly competitive lending market.
